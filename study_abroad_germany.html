Frequently Asked Questions
Do I need to take dMAT?
dMAT is required for some Indian Master's applicants whose bachelor's disciplines fall under the APS affected-fields list. Always confirm on the APS website for your specific degree classification.
How long does APS take?
APS verification typically takes several weeks; allow 6–12 weeks to be safe during peak intake periods.
Which documents for the visa?
Commonly required: passport, admission letter, APS certificate (if applicable), blocked account proof, health insurance, passport photos, and biometric appointment confirmation.
Step by step

Application Process

A complete, ordered walkthrough—from APS certification to German university enrollment.

SP
Study Abroad EditorialVerified by students at TU Munich & RWTH
12 min read
  • 01
    Apply for the APS Certificate
    Mandatory for most students from India. Start as early as possible—it can take several weeks. Visit aps-india.de.
  • 01A
    Check whether dMAT is required
    If you are applying for a Master's in Germany and your background falls under the APS India affected fields, you may need to take dMAT before APS verification. Confirm your exact eligibility on APS India or d-mat.de.
  • 02
    Research Universities & Deadlines
    Germany has Summer and Winter intakes. Use the DAAD portal to filter programs by subject, language, and fees. Eligibility varies per university—check each carefully.
  • 03
    Check Admission Requirements
    Review required bachelor's credits and subjects, IELTS/TOEFL scores, GRE requirements, and any program-specific entrance exams.
  • 04
    Prepare Required Documents
    APS Certificate · Academic transcripts & degree · IELTS/TOEFL/GRE scores · Letters of Recommendation · Statement of Purpose · German-style CV · Passport.
  • 05
    Apply via Uni-Assist or Direct Portals
    Most universities use Uni-Assist. Fees: ~€75 for the first university, ~€30 each additional. Some require certified documents by courier—check deadlines meticulously.
  • 06
    Monitor Application Status
    Check email frequently. Send a polite inquiry after the expected review period if you haven't heard back.
  • 07
    Complete University Enrollment
    Follow all enrollment instructions immediately upon receiving your offer. Submit any additional documents promptly.
  • 08
    Arrange Financial Proof & Health Insurance
    Open a blocked account (Expatrio, Coracle, or Fintiba) and arrange German health insurance. Then pay the semester contribution fee and finalize enrollment.
  • 09
    Start the Visa Process via CSP Portal
    Complete your application at digital.diplo.de/visa. Upload passport, admission letter, APS certificate, blocked account confirmation, and health insurance.
  • 10
    Respond to Visa Follow-Ups
    Monitor email daily. Respond quickly to requests for corrections or missing documents—delays here push back your biometric appointment.
  • 11
    Attend Biometric Appointment
    Bring all original documents, official passport photographs, and organized copies of everything. Arrive early.
  • 12
    Wait for Visa Approval
    Track your application status. Processing times vary by consulate workload and intake season.
  • 13
    Prepare for Your Move
    While waiting: shop for essentials, lock down accommodation, research your city and campus. You're nearly there.
Timeline
Begin APS and blocked account setup at least 6 months before your intended semester start. Visa processing alone can take 8–12 weeks at peak intake season.
2026 APS update

dMAT Guide

A detailed guide to the Digital Master Assessment Test for Indian Master's applicants in Germany, including eligibility, format, score use, and official timelines.

DM
Study Abroad EditorialBuilt from APS India and d-mat.de guidance
10 min read
What dMAT is
The Digital Master Assessment Test is an aptitude-based assessment introduced by APS India together with g.a.s.t. for selected Indian applicants. It does not replace APS; for some candidates it becomes an additional step in the APS process.
Who needs dMAT
Usually required
Indian students applying for Master's programs in Germany when the bachelor's background falls under the affected fields list, especially engineering, commerce, accounting, finance, economics, business, and management.
Usually not required
Bachelor's applicants, PhD applicants, Winter Semester 2026/27 applicants, exchange or double-degree applicants, and applicants who finished APS registration or submitted complete APS documents before 29 June 2026.
Computer Science note
If your Computer Science degree is classified as Engineering, you are generally in scope. For interdisciplinary degrees, the official APS affected-fields list decides the final answer.
How the process works
  • 01
    Register for APS
    Start with APS India. Your registration date and document submission timeline decide whether dMAT is part of your case.
  • 02
    Check if dMAT is mandatory
    Review the official affected-fields list and your APS timeline. If your case falls under the new rules, you complete dMAT before APS verification continues.
  • 03
    Take the digital exam
    The test is computer-based, in English, and administered at authorized centers in India. You receive a dMAT certificate after results are released.
  • 04
    Submit APS documents
    APS verification still happens as before. dMAT is an added requirement for selected applicants, not a replacement for document verification.
  • 05
    Use the certificate for admissions
    German universities may consider the score during admissions. Keep the certificate with your APS documents and application files.
Exam structure
Core Module
Measures logical reasoning, analytical thinking, pattern recognition, and problem solving. It is designed to test general cognitive ability, not memorized facts.
General Academic Module
Evaluates how well you apply academic knowledge to solve problems. The emphasis is on reasoning and application rather than rote learning.
Question style
Computer-based multiple-choice questions with one correct answer, delivered in English at authorized test centers in India.
Duration
Approximately 3.5 hours, including a break between modules.
Scoring and validity
Score
0 to 200 scale
Certificates include percentile rank, overall score, module scores, and the dMAT score. The average score is 100.
No pass/fail cutoff is stated for the dMAT score in the APS process.
Universities decide how much weight to give the result.
A low result does not automatically block APS verification.
Validity
Indefinite
The official dMAT site states the certificate is valid indefinitely.
Keep the certificate safe with your APS records.
Use it again for later applications if a university requests it.

Pre-visa requirements

The CSP portal process
CSP Portal
Germany now processes student visas via digital.diplo.de/visa. Create an account, select Student Visa, upload all documents, and submit online. The visa team reviews your upload—you may be asked for corrections. Respond quickly to avoid delays.

Germany's universities fall into three categories. Choosing the right type matters more than picking a famous name.

TU vs FH — the key decision
⭐ Recommended — AI / CS / Research
Technical University (TU)
TU Munich · RWTH Aachen · TU Darmstadt · KIT
Strong international reputation
Extensive research & PhD pathways
Industry-funded research projects
Strong alumni networks
Higher academic workload
More competitive admissions
Best for fast industry entry
Applied Sciences (FH/UAS)
Frankfurt · Munich · Darmstadt UAS
Practical, project-based curriculum
Mandatory internships in many programs
Strong industry connections
Smaller class sizes
Fewer PhD opportunities
Less internationally recognized than top TUs
Private Universities

GISMA, SRH, and IU International offer flexible admissions and more English-taught programs—but tuition ranges from €5,000 to €20,000+ per year. Some employers prefer public university graduates, so weigh the trade-off carefully.

The simple rule
If you want research, AI, or PhD pathways → Technical University. If your priority is industry experience and employment speed → University of Applied Sciences.

Application tips
  • Apply 6–12 months before intake—most programs have very early deadlines
  • Maintain a strong CGPA (8.0+/10 or equivalent)
  • Write a compelling SOP: Why Germany? Why this program? Your career goals?
  • Obtain strong Letters of Recommendation from professors
  • Highlight internships, research, volunteering, and leadership
  • Check university-funded scholarships (e.g. ELIZA)—fewer applicants, better odds
Work While You Study

Student Jobs in Germany

Non-EU students can work 140 full days or 280 half-days per year. Here's how to make it count.

Job types
Werkstudent ⭐
Field-related position. €14–25+/hr. Best for career growth and potential full-time conversion.
HiWi (Student Assistant)
Research, lab, teaching at university. €13–18/hr. Flexible and great for academic networking.
Part-Time Jobs
Supermarket, warehouse, restaurant, delivery. €12.82–16/hr. Easier without German skills.
Internship (Praktikum)
Mandatory or voluntary. €800–2000+/month. Many Werkstudent offers start here.
